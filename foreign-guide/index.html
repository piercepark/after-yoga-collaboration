05 · FAQ

Quick answers before you visit.

Tap any question to expand. For anything else, just send us a text or KakaoTalk.

Do I need any experience to join?
Not at all. Many of our members start as complete beginners. Instructors explain slowly and clearly, and you're encouraged to follow at your own pace. Beginner-friendly options exist in every program.
Are classes in English?
Classes are taught primarily in Korean, but yoga and pilates rely heavily on demonstration, so most members follow along visually without trouble. Several instructors use simple English cueing, and front-desk consultation is available in English.
What should I bring to my first class?
Just comfortable workout clothes. We provide mats, props, equipment, and showers. A water bottle is a good idea. If you'd like to do a free InBody body composition reading on arrival, just let us know.
How does the trial class work?
The trial is ₩30,000 for one class of your choice, including a studio tour and (optionally) a free InBody check. If you sign up for a membership afterwards, the ₩30,000 is fully credited toward your plan.
How do I book classes?
All bookings go through the StudioMate app (download via QR code on arrival or ask us for the link). Classes open 3 days in advance. Cancellations and reminders are all handled in-app.
Why do some yoga classes change each week?
Our yoga schedule rotates biweekly to keep training varied and balanced. Odd weeks (3, 5 in May) and even weeks (2, 4) have different class lineups at some time slots. Pilates does not rotate — same schedule every week.
Can I combine yoga and pilates?
Yes — our Combo membership lets you attend both at a 2:1 ratio (yoga : pilates), with the flexibility to choose per visit. It's the most popular plan for members who want strength and flexibility together.
Can I pause my membership?
Long-term plans (3+ months) can be held (paused) once per membership for up to 30 days. Class packs (5 / 10 classes) and stretching session packs cannot be held. Refunds follow the studio's standard pro-rated policy.
What is Passive Stretching — and who is it for?
Passive stretching is a 1-on-1 lying-down therapy where a licensed physical therapist moves your body through guided stretches — you stay relaxed the whole time. It sits between medical treatment and exercise. Great for desk-bound tension (tech neck, rounded shoulders), tight hips, recovery from training, or simply unwinding deeply. Designed by Bareun Stretching, a manual-therapy team trained on national athletes and audited by physicians.
How is the stretching service booked?
Stretching runs separately from group classes — by appointment only, not through the StudioMate app. Just message us with a preferred day/time and we'll find an open slot. A ₩39,000 trial session lets you experience it first; if you continue, it's credited toward your 5 / 10 / Family 30-session pack. May 2026 pre-sale rates apply through May 31 only (limited 30 slots).
